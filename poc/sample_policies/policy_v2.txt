BILLING POLICY: EVALUATION AND MANAGEMENT (E/M) SERVICES
Version: 2.0  |  Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Issuing Organization: Example Health Plan

---

SECTION 1: SCOPE
This policy governs reimbursement for Evaluation and Management (E/M) services billed
under CPT codes 99202–99215 for outpatient office visits and telehealth visits.

SECTION 2: COVERAGE CRITERIA
2.1 Covered Services
E/M services are covered when:
  - The service is medically necessary.
  - The provider is a participating in-network physician, mid-level practitioner, or
    licensed telehealth provider.
  - The visit is documented in the member's medical record.
  - For telehealth: the visit uses a HIPAA-compliant, real-time audio-visual platform.

2.2 Documentation Requirements
  - Beginning January 1, 2024, documentation must be based on Medical Decision Making (MDM)
    OR total time on the date of the encounter (face-to-face and non-face-to-face combined).
  - The three key component method (history, exam, MDM) is no longer required.
  - Physicians must document the total time spent on the day of the encounter when using
    time-based billing.
  - Copy-forward documentation from previous visits is prohibited without explicit attestation
    of review and update.

SECTION 3: CODING GUIDELINES
3.1 New Patient Codes (unchanged)
  - 99202: Straightforward MDM, 15–29 minutes
  - 99203: Low MDM, 30–44 minutes
  - 99204: Moderate MDM, 45–59 minutes
  - 99205: High MDM, 60–74 minutes

3.2 Established Patient Codes (unchanged)
  - 99211: Minimal, may not require physician presence
  - 99212: Straightforward MDM, 10–19 minutes
  - 99213: Low MDM, 20–29 minutes
  - 99214: Moderate MDM, 30–39 minutes
  - 99215: High MDM, 40–54 minutes

3.3 Telehealth Codes (NEW)
  - Telehealth E/M visits are billed with place of service (POS) code 02 (telehealth,
    patient not in their home) or POS 10 (telehealth, patient in their home).
  - Modifier 95 must be appended to all telehealth E/M codes.
  - Audio-only visits may be billed with modifier 93 when video is not technically feasible,
    subject to prior authorization.

SECTION 4: BILLING RESTRICTIONS
  - Same-day billing of E/M with procedure codes requires a separate, distinct service.
  - Modifier 25 must be appended to the E/M code when billed same-day as a procedure.
  - Split/shared visits between physician and APP must follow 2024 CMS guidelines; the
    substantive portion rule applies (the billing provider must perform >50% of total time).
  - Incident-to billing is not covered for new patients.
  - Copy-forward documentation without attestation will result in claim denial.

SECTION 5: REIMBURSEMENT
  - Reimbursement is based on the Medicare Physician Fee Schedule (MPFS) at 115% of Medicare
    allowable rates for participating providers (increased from 110%).
  - Non-participating providers are reimbursed at 80% of MPFS (unchanged).
  - Telehealth visits are reimbursed at parity with in-person visits through December 31, 2024.

SECTION 6: EXCLUDED SERVICES
  - Telephone-only consultations without a documented in-office OR telehealth component.
  - Services rendered by unlicensed personnel.
  - Administrative encounters not involving clinical evaluation.
  - Telehealth visits conducted via non-HIPAA-compliant platforms (e.g., standard FaceTime,
    standard Zoom without BAA).
